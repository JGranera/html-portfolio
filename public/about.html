About Me

Hi! My name is Jorge Granera. I’m happy to have you here! I was born and raised in the San Francisco Bay Area but moved to Los Angeles to pursue a career in filmmaking, specifically as a screenwriter. Having said that, I directed a short film that sold through Creative Artist Agency (CAA). My most recent accomplishment was being accepted into the Universal GTDI Writers Lab as one of six writers selected out of 2500 candidates. This opportunity allowed me to spend a year learning the film business from studio executives and producers. While it was a fun experience and I gained a ton of insight, I realized it wasn’t fulfilling my need to use my creativity to create a direct impact on people’s lives.

During my time at Universal, I heard a lot of talk in the industry about how tech was “infiltrating” Hollywood, especially with AI. There was a lot of fear surrounding this new integration of film and tech, but I’ve always believed in facing the unknown to better understand it rather than fearing it. As I did my research, I became intrigued by software engineering and its creative potential in pioneering new spaces. I quickly realized that becoming a software engineer was where I wanted to channel my passion. But I wondered, “How would my skills as a screenwriter translate to coding?”

One of the aspects that I love most about writing is my ability to problem solve, to look at a story and ask, “How can I make this better? How can I make this stronger? How can I deliver a better product to the audience?” I discovered that these same questions drive innovation in the tech industry. How can we create products that directly and immediately benefit people? Essentially, “How can we be of service to others?” When I started coding, I found a significant overlap between writing and coding. Both require problem-solving, strong attention to detail, and effective communication skills to convey and sell ideas. The key difference is that writing is a solitary experience, but work as a software engineer is highly collaborative. You’re part of a team, constantly collaborating to achieve a common goal. I thrive in that environment! Growing up playing sports and bass guitar in a punk hardcore band taught me the value of teamwork and collaboration, qualities I actively seek in any working environment.

Anyways, that’s a bit about me. I’m sure with time I’ll refine this further, but for now, this is the raw version.

Thanks for stopping by, and feel free to reach out! I'd love to connect with other like-minded individuals!
